Message from the President: Community of Caring

Posted by Tim Keane | President | 2 min read

Dear Webster University Global Community:

According to research from the Peace Institute, the world is experiencing a surge in armed conflicts not seen since World War II. As the largest global university in terms of locations across the world, these violent times impact the Webster University Global Community in tangible, often very personal, ways. Just this week, we learned that a Webster student, Captain Cody Khork, was one of the American soldiers killed in Kuwait. We have reached out to Captain Khork's family to offer condolences.

Webster’s vision, mission and core values stand in stark contrast to violence, hatred and exclusion - period. Perhaps our "Community of Care" value articulates most clearly our intentional way of being:

We understand that we are interconnected and greater than the sum of our parts. By leading with love, Webster creates a community that is the heart of learning and growth. Through a shared ethic of care and compassion, we promote a culture of belonging that empowers each person to contribute their unique perspectives. By embracing dialogue, mutual respect, and social responsibility, we nurture individuals who contribute to the common good within our campus communities and on a global scale.

There are thousands of Webster employees and students from countries impacted by the escalating violence across the Middle East who are feeling high levels of stress. Webster offers support resources to our Community. For students worldwide, please visit these sites for specific options that might help: Protocall and TogetherAll. For a comprehensive list of services available to all employees and students, please contact the Human Resources office at your location.

There are other ways to support one another. If you know someone in our Community who might be personally impacted by violence, hatred, or exclusion, whether in the form of these global events on social media or any other way, please reach out to let them know you care. Sometimes all it takes is hearing a friendly voice.

Wishing everyone personal peace,

Tim Keane
President
